# Part 1 — Client Sovereign Hub (everyday signers)

## "What's Next?" Card *(the top of your dashboard)*

**What it is:** A single colored card at the top of your dashboard that tells you the one most important thing you should do right now.

**The aha:** Imagine a personal assistant who looks at your expiring documents, your half-finished notarizations, and your reminders — and instead of dumping a to-do list on you, says *"Right now, do this one thing."* That's this card.

**How to use it:** Just look at it. Click the button. Done.

**Pro tip:** If the card says "You're all caught up," it really means it. Go take the rest of the day off.

---

## Quick Seal

**What it is:** Drag and drop any file. We hash it, timestamp it on the Hedera blockchain, and give you back a receipt. Takes about 8 seconds.

**The aha:** It's like getting a notary's stamp on a document without leaving your desk *and* without needing a notary. The blockchain proves the document existed in this exact form, at this exact second, forever. Anyone in the world can verify it — they don't have to trust us.

**How to use it:**
1. Drag your PDF onto the Quick Seal card
2. Click "Seal Document"
3. You get a verification link you can paste anywhere — email, contract, deed

**Pro tip:** Use Quick Seal for *evidence*, not for documents that need a wet-ink notary. Examples: proof you sent a manuscript before the publisher saw it, proof a design was yours before a copycat appeared, proof of a price quote at a specific moment.

## Request Notarization

**What it is:** The full RON (Remote Online Notarization) ceremony. You meet a real, commissioned notary on video. They verify your ID. You sign. They seal it. Legally binding in every state that recognizes RON.

**The aha:** This replaces the entire "drive to the UPS Store at 4:45pm before they close" experience. You can do it from a beach in Bali.

**How to use it:**
1. Upload your document
2. Pick a notary from the marketplace (or let us auto-match)
3. Verify your ID (driver's license + KBA quiz)
4. Get on the video call when scheduled
5. Sign with your finger / stylus / mouse
6. Receive your sealed PDF + blockchain receipt

**Pro tip:** If you have 5+ documents to notarize, don't do 5 separate sessions — use **Bulk Notarization** below.

## Bulk Notarization

**What it is:** Notarize an entire stack of documents in a single ceremony.

**The aha:** Real estate closings, estate planning, corporate filings — these are dozens of documents each. Bulk lets you do them all in one 30-minute session instead of one-by-one.

**How to use it:** Same as Request Notarization but upload up to 50 documents. The notary walks through each one with you in sequence.

**Pro tip:** Pre-label your documents in the order you want to sign them. The notary will go through them in upload order.

## AI Document Generator

**What it is:** Type what you need in plain English ("a power of attorney for my mother to manage my apartment while I'm overseas") and the AI drafts the legal document.

**The aha:** Drafting legal documents used to cost $300–$800 in attorney time for a single form. Now it's 90 seconds and free for our subscribers. You still get an attorney to review for high-stakes documents, but you skip the "blank page" cost.

**How to use it:**
1. Click AI Doc Generator
2. Describe what you need in 1–3 sentences
3. AI drafts; you review and edit
4. Send to a notary or keep as a draft

**Pro tip:** The AI is best at *standard* documents (POA, wills, NDAs, leases, deeds). For complex IP or M&A documents, generate a first draft and hand it to an attorney — you'll cut their billable time by 60%.

## AI Summarizer

**What it is:** Upload a contract. Get a 5-bullet summary, every dollar amount, every deadline, and a "what could go wrong" risk paragraph.

**The aha:** A 40-page mortgage agreement compresses into something you can read in 90 seconds and actually understand. Stops you from signing something you didn't read.

**How to use it:** Upload PDF → click Summarize → read.

**Pro tip:** Always summarize a contract *before* the notary session. The notary can't give legal advice, but understanding the contract beforehand saves you from a panic mid-ceremony.

## Doc Compare

**What it is:** Upload version 1 and version 2 of any document. See exactly what changed — additions, deletions, financial figures, dates.

**The aha:** Lawyers call this "redlining" and charge by the hour to do it. You do it in 4 seconds.

**How to use it:** Upload v1 → upload v2 → see a side-by-side highlight diff with the AI's plain-English explanation of what each change means in practice.

**Pro tip:** When your attorney sends you a "minor revision," run it through Doc Compare before you sign. "Minor" sometimes means "we slipped in a clause that changes the deal."

## Asset Vault

**What it is:** Your digital safe-deposit box. Encrypted storage for wills, deeds, beneficiaries, life-insurance policies, and the kind of documents that *must* be findable when something goes wrong.

**The aha:** When someone dies, their family spends weeks hunting for the will, the trust, the safe-deposit-box key, the property deeds. The Asset Vault solves that. You designate beneficiaries. When you pass, they get a one-time access link.

**How to use it:**
1. Upload your wills, deeds, policies into the vault
2. Designate beneficiaries (email + relationship)
3. Optionally set a "dead-man's switch" — if you don't check in for 90 days, beneficiaries are notified
4. Documents stay encrypted; beneficiaries can only see them after the trigger fires

**Pro tip:** Add your *digital* assets too — crypto wallet recovery phrases (sealed), online account inventories, password manager backups. This is the #1 thing financial planners ask for and nobody actually does.

## My Requests

**What it is:** Your active assignments — accepted but not yet completed.

**The aha:** Your personal queue of paying work. Each row shows the signer, the document type, and a one-click "Start session" or "Join session" button.

**How to use it:** Tap "Start session" → enter video room → run the ceremony.

**Pro tip:** Run the AI Co-pilot (next section) *before* you click Start. It pre-loads everything you need.

## AI Co-pilot *(the killer feature for notaries)*

**What it is:** An AI that reads the document, analyzes the signer's information, and gives you a pre-ceremony briefing.

**The aha:** Before you greet the signer, you already know: what type of document this is, whether there are red flags (mismatched names, dates that don't add up, missing clauses), what compliance risks exist in this jurisdiction, and a 5-item checklist of what to verify during the ceremony. You walk into the call looking like you've been doing this for 20 years.

**How to use it:**
1. Click any request → "Analyze Request"
2. Read the summary, risk level, key highlights, and flags
3. Optionally click "Generate" to auto-fill your journal entry
4. Start the session

**Pro tip:** If the Co-pilot flags "HIGH" risk, slow down. Most fraud attempts are caught here — not in the camera.

## Notary Journal

**What it is:** Your statutorily-required logbook. Every notarization gets a journal entry: signer's name, ID type, document description, date, fee.

**The aha:** Most notaries keep a paper journal that fills up, gets lost, and is a nightmare to produce in court. NotaryChain's journal is **auto-filled by AI**, blockchain-anchored, and exportable to PDF on demand. Audit-ready in one click.

**How to use it:** The Co-pilot generates the entry automatically. Review it, click Save. Done.

**Pro tip:** Export your journal monthly as a SOC2-compliance habit. Lawyers love this.

## Digital Seal

**What it is:** Your unique cryptographic seal — the digital equivalent of your physical embosser.

**The aha:** Your seal is signed with **Ed25519 cryptography**, anchored on Hedera, and **impossible to forge**. A counterfeit "John Notary" seal would be detected by any verification check anywhere in the world.

**How to use it:** Set it up once during onboarding. Every notarization automatically uses it. You can rotate keys if compromised.

**Pro tip:** Print your public-key fingerprint on your business card. It's a credibility flex that other notaries don't have.
